Indicative Pricing Output — HUM
TenorYears to maturity of the new bond. 2/5/7/10/20/30Y are standard IG benchmark tenors. Ask Tigress AI ›
TreasuryRisk-free yield from the live US Treasury curve at this tenor (Bloomberg GT*Govt). Shifted by the Treasury Shock slider. Ask Tigress AI ›
Peer SpreadCredit spread (bps) from the NSS curve fit through peer + issuer bonds, floored at issuer\'s observed curve. Shifted by IG Spread Shock slider. Ask Tigress AI ›
Credit AdjAuto: issuer rating minus peer-average rating, times a per-tenor multiplier (3 bps/notch at 2Y → 8 bps/notch at 30Y). Positive = issuer weaker than peers. Ask Tigress AI ›
Issuer AdjAnalyst-judgment premium for issuer-specific risks not captured by rating (e.g. CreditWatch, debut issuer). Slider value × per-tenor scale (0.57× at 2Y → 1.5× at 30Y). Ask Tigress AI ›
NICNew Issue Concession — extra spread to lure investors from secondary to a new bond. Slider value + auto big-deal bump (+1 over $1.5B, +3 over $2B). Typical IG range: 3–7 bps. Ask Tigress AI ›
Final SpreadAll-in spread to Treasury you would quote to the market: Peer + Credit Adj + Issuer Adj + NIC. Ask Tigress AI ›
YieldAll-in indicative yield: Treasury (with shock) + Final Spread/100. The actual rate the issuer pays. Ask Tigress AI ›
IPTInitial Price Talk = Final Spread + 12 bps. The first publicly announced spread, deliberately wider to attract initial orders. Ask Tigress AI ›
GuidanceFinal Spread + 5 bps. Where you expect to land after IPT, tightened based on order book demand. Ask Tigress AI ›
